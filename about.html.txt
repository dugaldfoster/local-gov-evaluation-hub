    <section class="main">
      <h2 class="section-title">What “good” looks like</h2>
      <p class="section-lede">
        Strong local evaluation is less about fancy techniques and more about clarity, credibility, and follow-through.
      </p>

      <div class="grid">
        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Clear purpose</h3>
          <p>Everyone agrees the evaluation is answering a specific set of questions for a real decision.</p>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Commissioning</span><span class="tag">Governance</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Proportionate design</h3>
          <p>You choose the strongest feasible approach and document what it can/can’t claim.</p>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Feasibility</span><span class="tag">Quality</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Data you can actually get</h3>
          <p>Measures are defined, owned, and collected in time to matter.</p>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Data</span><span class="tag">Delivery</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Ethics and safeguarding</h3>
          <p>Consent, privacy, and risk are built in—not bolted on at the end.</p>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Ethics</span><span class="tag">GDPR</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Learning loops</h3>
          <p>Findings are discussed early and often, not just in a final report.</p>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Learning</span><span class="tag">Improvement</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Actionable outputs</h3>
          <p>Recommendations have owners, dates, and a mechanism for tracking decisions.</p>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Impact</span><span class="tag">Accountability</span></div>
        </article>
      </div>

      <h2 class="section-title" style="margin-top:26px;">How to use this hub</h2>
      <div class="grid">
        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>If you’re commissioning</h3>
          <p>Start with the decision, the timeline, and the minimum evidence needed. Then pick a design.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Write evaluation questions (max 5)</li>
            <li>Agree what success looks like</li>
            <li>Decide what you can resource</li>
          </ul>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>If you’re delivering</h3>
          <p>Start with logic model + data sources. Build a short analysis plan early.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Define measures and baselines</li>
            <li>Agree comparison / counterfactual approach</li>
            <li>Set reporting rhythm (fortnightly/monthly)</li>
          </ul>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>If you need quick credibility</h3>
          <p>Focus on transparency: state limitations, triangulate sources, and show sensitivity checks.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Document assumptions</li>
            <li>Use mixed methods where possible</li>
            <li>Keep an audit trail</li>
          </ul>
        </article>
      </div>
