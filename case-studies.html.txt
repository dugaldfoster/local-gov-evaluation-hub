    <section class="main">
      <h2 class="section-title">Reusable case-study patterns</h2>
      <p class="section-lede">These are intentionally generic, so you can tailor them to your local programmes and data.</p>

      <div class="grid">
        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Pattern: Pilot → iterate → scale</h3>
          <p>Small pilot with tight process evaluation, then scale with stronger outcome tracking.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Clear eligibility + referral pathways</li>
            <li>Implementation learning in weeks, not months</li>
            <li>Decision gate: scale / adapt / stop</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Agile</span><span class="tag">Learning loop</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Pattern: Targeted support for a high-need group</h3>
          <p>Combine admin data with lived experience to test whether support reduces avoidable demand.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Define “need” consistently</li>
            <li>Track pathways (what services used, when)</li>
            <li>Look for displacement and unintended effects</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Equity</span><span class="tag">Demand management</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Pattern: Service redesign</h3>
          <p>Measure whether the new model improves timeliness, quality, and outcomes—without worsening access.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Use run charts / time series</li>
            <li>Triangulate staff + user feedback</li>
            <li>Document implementation variance</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Operations</span><span class="tag">Quality</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Pattern: Place-based partnership programme</h3>
          <p>Multiple actors, shared outcomes. Use contribution-based approaches and joint measurement plans.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Shared theory of change</li>
            <li>Contribution story + evidence map</li>
            <li>Governance for data sharing</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Systems</span><span class="tag">Partnership</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Pattern: Commissioned provider outcomes</h3>
          <p>Specify outcomes well, then monitor quality and gaming risk.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Define outcomes and verification</li>
            <li>Audit samples, not everything</li>
            <li>Include qualitative checks for experience</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Commissioning</span><span class="tag">Assurance</span></div>
        </article>

        <article class="card" data-card>
          <h3>Pattern: Prevention / early help</h3>
          <p>Benefits appear later. Use intermediate outcomes and long-term modelling carefully.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Agree proxy outcomes (short-term signals)</li>
            <li>Track retention and reach</li>
            <li>Be cautious with monetisation claims</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="meta"><span class="tag">Prevention</span><span class="tag">Long-term</span></div>
        </article>
      </div>
