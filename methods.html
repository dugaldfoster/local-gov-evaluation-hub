Methods

A practical menu of evaluation approaches, from “quick and useful” to more rigorous causal designs. The best method is the one that answers the decision question credibly within your constraints.

Core approaches

Use the cards as “building blocks” for an evaluation plan.

Process evaluation

What was delivered, to whom, how well, and why?

  • Fidelity, reach, dose, quality
  • Barriers/enablers, implementation lessons
  • Often uses interviews, observation, admin data
LearningImplementation

Before/after (pre-post)

Did outcomes change after the programme started?

  • Simple, fast, but weak on attribution
  • Improve with multiple time points
  • Add sensitivity checks for external shocks
QuickLow attribution

Comparison group

Compare with similar people/areas not receiving the intervention.

  • Matching (propensity score, exact, coarsened)
  • Choose comparators before looking at results
  • Check balance and spillovers
ImpactQuasi-experimental

Difference-in-differences

Compare changes over time between intervention and comparison groups.

  • Key assumption: parallel trends
  • Works well with routine datasets
  • Use placebo tests where possible
Causal-ishTime series

Interrupted time series

Use many data points to detect a change in level or trend after intervention.

  • Strong when you have frequent measures
  • Adjust for seasonality
  • Consider concurrent policy changes
ImpactRoutine data

Value for money (VfM)

Do benefits justify costs, and compared to what else you could do?

  • Cost-effectiveness (cost per outcome)
  • Cost-benefit / social return (monetised benefits)
  • Sensitivity analysis is non-negotiable

Minimum viable evaluation plan (1–2 pages)

Questions

Limit to what you can answer well.

  • Implementation: what happened and why?
  • Outcomes: who changed, how much?
  • Equity: who benefited, who didn’t?

Measures & data sources

Define each metric (unit, frequency, owner, extraction method).

  • Admin data / performance systems
  • Surveys, interviews, focus groups
  • Neighbouring area benchmarks

Analysis & reporting

Say what you’ll do before you see results.

  • Primary outcomes + subgroup checks
  • Handling missing data
  • Update rhythm and decision dates
